Momo is a staple in Nepali cuisine, typically filled with a mixture of ground meat and spices, wrapped in a thin dough wrapper, and steamed to perfection. We'll make a classic version with a buffalo meat filling, but feel free to substitute with chicken or vegetables if you prefer.

**Ingredients:**

For the dough:

* 2 cups all-purpose flour
* 1/2 teaspoon salt
* 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
* 1/4 cup lukewarm water

For the filling:

* 1 pound ground buffalo meat (or substitute with ground chicken or vegetables)
* 1 onion, finely chopped
* 2 cloves garlic, minced
* 1 tablespoon grated ginger
* 1 tablespoon soy sauce
* 1 tablespoon sesame oil
* 1 teaspoon ground cumin
* 1 teaspoon ground coriander
* Salt, to taste
* 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
* Chopped cilantro, for garnish

**Let's start with the dough:**

1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, salt, and baking powder.
2. Gradually add the lukewarm water, mixing the dough until it comes together in a smooth, pliable ball.
3. Knead the dough for about 5-7 minutes, until it's elastic and doesn't stick to your hands.
4. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.

**Now, let's prepare the filling:**

1. In a separate mixing bowl, combine the ground meat (or substitute), chopped onion, minced garlic, grated ginger, soy sauce, sesame oil, cumin, coriander, salt, and black pepper. Mix everything together until just combined. Be careful not to overmix.
2. Taste the filling and adjust the seasoning if needed.

**Assembling the Momo:**

1. Divide the rested dough into small balls, about the size of a small egg.
2. Roll out each ball into a thin circle, about 3-4 inches in diameter.
3. Place a tablespoon or two of the filling in the center of each dough circle.
4. Fold the dough over the filling, forming a half-moon shape, and press the edges together to seal the Momo. Make sure they're tightly sealed to prevent the filling from escaping during steaming.

**Steaming the Momo:**

1. Arrange the Momo in a steamer basket, leaving a little space between each one to prevent them from sticking together.
2. Steam the Momo over boiling water for 15-20 minutes, or until they're cooked through and fluffy.

**Serving:**

1. Serve the Momo hot with a dipping sauce of your choice. A classic Nepali chutney made with tomatoes, chilies, garlic, and cilantro is a great accompaniment.
